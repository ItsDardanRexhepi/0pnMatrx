# Sponsoring 0pnMatrx

## Why Sponsor

0pnMatrx exists because of a simple belief: everyone deserves a balanced chance at life, and technology should remove barriers, not create them. The platform is free. It will always be free. But free does not mean costless.

Running 0pnMatrx takes real money:

- **Infrastructure costs.** Servers, bandwidth, blockchain node operations, database hosting, CDN delivery, and monitoring. These run 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, and they scale with every new user who joins the platform.
- **Development.** Building new features, maintaining existing ones, reviewing community contributions, fixing bugs, and keeping dependencies up to date. The core team works on 0pnMatrx full-time.
- **iOS app maintenance.** Apple's ecosystem demands continuous attention -- new OS versions, App Store review compliance, Swift updates, and device compatibility testing. Keeping the iOS app functional and approved is an ongoing cost.
- **Security and audits.** Smart contract audits, penetration testing, and security reviews are not optional for a platform that handles real assets and real identity data.

Sponsorship is what makes this sustainable. When you sponsor 0pnMatrx, you are not paying for your own access -- you are paying to keep the doors open for everyone else. You are funding the infrastructure that lets someone in a developing country access DeFi loans with no bank account, or an independent artist enforce royalties on their work forever.

That is what your money does. It keeps the free tier free.

## How Funds Are Used

We believe in full transparency about where sponsorship money goes. Here is the breakdown:

### Infrastructure (40%)
- Cloud hosting and server costs
- Blockchain node operations and RPC endpoints
- Database hosting and backups
- CDN and content delivery
- Monitoring, alerting, and incident response tooling
- Domain registration and SSL certificates

### Development (35%)
- Core platform feature development
- Bug fixes and maintenance
- Code reviews and community contribution management
- Dependency updates and security patches
- Testing infrastructure and CI/CD pipeline costs
- Documentation

### iOS App Maintenance (15%)
- Apple Developer Program annual fee
- App Store submission and review cycles
- Swift and SwiftUI updates for new iOS versions
- Device compatibility testing across iPhone and iPad models
- Performance optimization and crash monitoring

### Security and Audits (10%)
- Smart contract security audits by third-party firms
- Penetration testing
- Dependency vulnerability scanning
- Security incident response preparation

These percentages are targets. Actual spending varies month to month based on platform needs, but we publish real numbers in our quarterly transparency reports.

## How to Become a Sponsor

There are three ways to sponsor 0pnMatrx:

The easiest way. GitHub handles everything -- billing, receipts, and tier management.

1. Go to [github.com/sponsors/ItsDardanRexhepi](https://github.com/sponsors/ItsDardanRexhepi)

Best for organizations that need invoices, expense tracking, or fiscal transparency.

1. Go to [opencollective.com/openmatrix](https://opencollective.com/openmatrix)

- Visit [openmatrix.io/sponsor](https://openmatrix.io/sponsor)

## Transparency Commitment

0pnMatrx is built on the principle that openness creates trust. That principle applies to our finances too.

**What we commit to:**

- **Quarterly financial reports.** Every three months, we publish a report showing total sponsorship income, how it was spent, and what it funded. These reports are posted publicly in the repository.
- **Open Collective visibility.** All transactions through Open Collective are visible to anyone at any time. No exceptions.
- **Honest communication.** If we need more funding, we will say so directly. If we have a surplus, we will say that too and explain how we plan to use it.
- **No hidden deals.** Every sponsorship tier and its benefits are documented here. No sponsor receives undisclosed benefits or preferential treatment beyond what their tier provides.

We ask people to trust us with their money. The least we can do is show them exactly where it goes.
